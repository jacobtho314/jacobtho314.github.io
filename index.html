Bio

I am a 2nd year MSR student at Carnegie Mellon University, where I am fortunately advised by Yonatan Bisk.

I'm most interested in integrating language models with program induction to build coherent, theory-based world models that learn new skills from a few examples and update themselves through minimal, local repairs without catastrophic forgetting. Given fundamental limitations in LLM compositionality, sample efficiency, and out-of-distribution generalization, I have pivoted to working on bridging pretrained language modeling with structured probabilistic representations for more robust, generalizable world modeling.

I was first introduced to research at MIT's Computational Reactor Physics Group during my freshman year at Harvard, and began working with machine learning at Caltech's Autonomous Robotics and Controls Lab under Soon-Jo Chung.

I will be applying to PhD Programs this fall! (2025)

Embodied Semantic Memory

Jacob Thompson, Haochen Zhang, Quanting Xie, Fan Yang, Yonatan Bisk

Neither humans nor Visual Language Models (VLMs) can effectively reason over increasingly vast scene contexts; attempting to process hours of raw video is both computationally prohibitive and prone to attention collapse. To achieve genuine large-scale scene understanding, embodied agents must instead continuously summarize and abstract their experiences into a compact, structured representation. We propose Embodied Semantic Memory (ESM), an agentic framework enabling VLMs to actively decide what spatial, semantic, and temporal information to store in a hierarchical graph. We first validate ESM on the standard Open-EQA benchmark, where it achieves parity with human baselines on episodic queries. To stress-test the paradigm on longer horizons, we introduce Campus-Bench, a continuous 2-hour real-world egocentric walkthrough containing over 3,000 queries that require reasoning across district-scale environments. On this benchmark, ESM demonstrates superior reasoning capabilities compared to naive memory baselines, while matching or exceeding the accuracy of brute-force long-context approaches with drastically reduced computational overhead. By shifting the focus from ever-longer context windows toward agentic, structured memory, we outline a path toward more generally useful embodied agents.
